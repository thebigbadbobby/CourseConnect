								<h1>
	Jewish Studies B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>The interdisciplinary program in Jewish studies introduces students to the study of classical, medieval, and modern Jewish cultures and to the range of disciplines that bear upon the field.</p>

<p>The Jewish studies major and minor offer students the chance to gain knowledge and skills in a variety of contexts in various aspects of Jewish culture, with special reference (though not limited) to modern issues. The major and minor will help them prepare to move successfully into graduate programs in a variety of disciplines, especially in humanities, social sciences, and pre-professional programs, and will provide students with a grounding in materials fundamental to a liberal arts education. This program connects with a range of disciplines and programs on the UCSC campus that explore the meanings of modernity; at the same time, this program will help students develop analytical tools, methodological versatility, and critical literacy.</p>

<p>Note that modernity here is not defined as Europe or North America; the Jewish studies program intends to speak to modernity as a global phenomenon, dealing with sites where Jews lived. In the 19th and 20th centuries Jews were widely spread across the globe and often played active and influential roles in their communities. These include such places as China, India, Latin America, North Africa, the Middle East, as well as Europe&nbsp;and North America.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>The Jewish studies program is administered by the Department of History. Students in this program receive advising support from the <a href="https://history.ucsc.edu/undergraduate/undergraduate-advising/index.html" target="_blank">history undergraduate program coordinator</a>.</p>

<p>Transfer students are encouraged to consult the &quot;Transfer Information and Policy&quot; information included below.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p><strong>Jewish History and Culture</strong></p>

<p>Identify and interpret major events, figures, and topics in Jewish history and culture.</p>

<p><strong>Holocaust</strong></p>

<p>Demonstrate thorough knowledge of the Holocaust as a historical phenomenon.</p>

<p>Develop a nuanced understanding of the cultural dimensions of the Holocaust through an analysis of literature, films, art, and/or music.</p>

<p><strong>Jewish Languages</strong></p>

<p>Develop basic competency in Hebrew (modern or Biblical) or Yiddish.</p>

<p><strong>Critical Analysis</strong></p>

<p>Distinguish between primary and secondary sources.</p>

<p>Evaluate competing interpretations and multiple narratives of the past.</p>

<p>Analyze Jewish cultural sources, including literary texts, films, and music within a broader, cross-cultural context.</p>

<p><strong>Effective Communication</strong></p>

<p>Present clear and compelling arguments, based on critical analysis of diverse literary, historical, film, and/or musical sources, and effectively communicate interpretations in written essays and/or other media.</p>

<p><strong>Scope</strong></p>

<p>Classical Period.</p>

<p>Modern Period.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Major Qualification Policy and Declaration Process
		</h3><a id="mqual"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Major Qualification
		</h4><p>Undeclared students may declare the Jewish studies major at any time. While specific courses are not required in order to declare, students will have ideally completed some or all of the lower-division requirements.</p><a id="decproc"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			How to Declare a Major
		</h4><ol>
	<li>Fill out a <a href="https://jewishstudies.ucsc.edu/undergraduate/JWST_major_worksheet.pdf" target="_blank">Jewish Studies Major Planning Worksheet</a>. Include Jewish studies courses you&#39;ve completed, are currently taking, and plan on taking in the future.</li>
	<li>Meet with a <a href="https://jewishstudies.ucsc.edu/undergraduate/jewish-major/declaring-major.html#jwstadviser" target="_blank">Jewish studies faculty adviser</a> to review and discuss your proposed plan of study. Obtain their signature on your worksheet.</li>
	<li>Submit your approved Jewish Studies Major Planning Worksheet and a completed <a href="https://advising.ucsc.edu/planning/your-major/declaration/docs/major-declaration.pdf" target="_blank">UCSC Petition for Major/Minor Declaration</a> to the <a href="https://history.ucsc.edu/undergraduate/undergraduate-advising/index.html" target="_blank">history undergraduate program coordinator</a> for processing.</li>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Transfer Admission Screening Policy
		</h4><p>Students planning to apply&nbsp;in this major are not required to complete specific major preparation courses for consideration of admission to UC Santa Cruz.</p>

<p>Transfer students entering at the junior level can readily finish all of the Jewish Studies major requirements within two years, even if they haven&rsquo;t completed any preparatory coursework prior to transferring. Some background in history and in literary analysis and interpretation is desirable, but not essential. Since this is an interdisciplinary major, preparatory work in art history, philosophy, or politics, depending on the student&rsquo;s interests, may also be useful.</p>

<p>Transfer students&nbsp;are encouraged&nbsp;to complete the <a href="https://admission.universityofcalifornia.edu/admission-requirements/transfer-requirements/general-education-igetc/igetc/" target="_blank">Intersegmental General Education Transfer Curriculum (IGETC)</a>&nbsp;or UCSC general education requirements in preparation for transfer to UC Santa Cruz.</p>
<table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Getting Started at UCSC as a Transfer Student
		</h4><p>Transfer students are advised to complete the lower-division Jewish studies course requirements, including the language requirement, by the end of their first year. The upper-division courses required for this major may be taken simultaneously with the required lower-division courses. Please refer to the sample academic plan provided below.</p>

<p>Transfer students may declare the Jewish studies major during their first quarter of enrollment at UCSC.</p>

    <li>
    <p class="sc-BodyText">Related courses not currently on the pre-approved Jewish studies course list (limit of two)</p>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Study Abroad
		</h3><p>The program in Jewish studies encourages students to take advantage of the <a href="http://uc.eap.ucop.edu/" target="_blank">UC Education Abroad Program (EAP)</a>. The University of California has developed educational opportunities abroad in conjunction with the Jerusalem Study Center at The Hebrew University of Jerusalem. Subject to the limitations described in the &quot;Course Substitution Policy&quot; section, up to three courses from EAP may be applied toward the Jewish studies major requirements.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Honors
		</h3><p>All students who announce candidacy during the academic year are reviewed for honors or highest honors in the major. The Jewish studies faculty advisers determine honors based upon courses applied toward the Jewish studies major. Performance in courses taken elsewhere and being transferred toward the major will be considered when applicable. The minimum standard applied is excellence in most courses for honors, and excellence in all courses for highest honors. Summer, fall, and winter graduates will be reviewed at the end of each of their respective quarters. Spring graduates will be reviewed as of the spring announcement of candidacy deadline.</p>
