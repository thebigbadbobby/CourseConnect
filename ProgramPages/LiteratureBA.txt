								<h1>
	Literature B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>The Literature Department at UC Santa Cruz&nbsp;fosters innovative and comparative approaches to reading and interpretation in courses&nbsp; in a variety of languages that encompass traditional literary history, cross-cultural inquiry, current theoretical debates, and new media.</p>

<p>Literature majors learn to read, think, and write critically. These skills have wide applicability, and offer pathways into related disciplines such as history, philosophy, psychology, sociology, anthropology, politics, and the history of art and visual culture. Literature majors enter a variety of careers ranging from law and journalism to management, government, international studies, publishing, technical writing, and teaching at all levels. The literature major prepares students for careers in digital storytelling and electronic media; film, television, and video; theater and the visual arts; and many other fields.</p>

<p>The Literature Department faculty requires that all literature majors have basic proficiency in a second language. Proficiency in more than one language enhances understanding of any literature and culture. Graduate programs in literature and other humanities disciplines generally require competence in at least one language other than English.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>Faculty advisers are available in the Literature Department office throughout each academic term; students may make appointments in advance to meet with them. Staff advisers are available by appointment and on a drop-in basis. Students are encouraged to consult with an adviser once per quarter.</p>

<p>Transfer students should also consult the section Transfer Information and Policy.</p>

<p>Advisers for the literature program are available at <a href="mailto:litdept@ucsc.edu">litdept@ucsc.edu</a>, (831) 459-4778, or by visiting the Literature Department office in Humanities 1, room 303.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Getting Started in the Major
		</h3><p>All students considering a literature major or minor should consult with staff and/or faculty advisers at the Literature Department office as early as possible, and declare the major or minor before the end of their sophomore year. Transfer students are urged to declare their major or minor during their first quarter at UCSC, and should also consult the section Transfer Information and Policy.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>Following is a summary of the program learning outcomes for the Literature B.A.:</p>

<p>Students who complete the literature major should emerge with the following knowledge and skills:</p>

<ol>
	<li>A capacity for critical analysis of texts that is attentive to:
	<ul>
		<li>the formal structures, genres, and rhetorical strategies of different kinds of writing, public discourse, and media;</li>
		<li>the historical and social contexts of such texts;</li>
		<li>the role cultural and linguistic difference play in the interpretation of texts read in translation and in a language not one&#39;s own.</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li>Effective written communication that demonstrates the following abilities:
	<ul>
		<li>to evaluate multiple interpretations of texts;</li>
		<li>to write effective argumentative prose;</li>
		<li>to situate texts in relation to a critical/theoretical tradition;</li>
		<li>to design and initiate a substantive independent project of research or creative activity.</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
	<li>Experience reading a variety of texts:
	<ul>
		<li>from a breadth of world cultures and traditions;</li>
		<li>from different historical periods, including the pre-modern;</li>
		<li>originally written in languages other than English.</li>
	</ul>
	</li>
</ol>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Major Qualification Policy and Declaration Process
		</h3><a id="mqual"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Major Qualification

<table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Critical Approaches Requirements
		</h4><p>&bull; Six courses, one satisfying each of the six critical approaches to the study of literature.</p>

<p>While most upper-division courses have two critical approaches designations, no single course may be used to satisfy more than one critical approaches requirement.</p>

<p><strong>Canons:</strong> The study of influential authors or works, and their critical afterlives: what books get read, which are forgotten, and how is that decided?</p>

<p><strong>Genres:</strong> The study of fiction, poetry, drama, epic, testimonio, etc., across time and space: what happens when we classify together works of similar form?</p>

<p><strong>Geographies:</strong> The study of local, regional, national, transnational, or global contexts: how do we use notions of place to group texts together?</p>

<p><strong>Histories:</strong> The study of texts through socially or aesthetically defined periods or movements: how do historical pressures affect literature&rsquo;s possibilities?</p>

<p><strong>Media:</strong> The study of the written word as one medium among others: what can we learn from the analysis of visual, performative, sonic, filmic, and other media?</p>

<p><strong>Power and Subjectivities: </strong>The study of human and other subjects as individuals and in collective groups: who has the power to speak, write, and read under different social conditions?</p>

<p>The course descriptions in the General Catalog specify the critical approaches satisfied by each course.</p>

<p>These courses may also satisfy distribution requirements (below).</p>

<p>A list of annual course offerings indicating critical approaches and distribution codes for each course is available in the department office and on <a href="https://literature.ucsc.edu/courses/index.php" target="_blank">the Literature Department website</a>.</p>

		</h4><p>&bull; Six courses, one satisfying each of the six critical approaches to the study of Literature.</p>

<p>While most upper-division courses have two critical approaches designations, no single course may be used to satisfy more than one critical approaches requirement.</p>

<p><strong>Canons:</strong> The study of influential authors or works, and their critical afterlives: what books get read, which are forgotten, and how is that decided?</p>

<p><strong>Genres:</strong> The study of fiction, poetry, drama, epic, testimonio, etc., across time and space: what happens when we classify together works of similar form?</p>

<p><strong>Media:</strong> The study of the written word as one medium among others: what can we learn from the analysis of visual, performative, sonic, filmic, and other media?</p>

<p><strong>Power and Subjectivities: </strong>The study of human and other subjects as individuals and in collective groups: who has the power to speak, write, and read under different social conditions?</p>

<p>Students in the creative writing concentration will fulfill the genres critical approaches requirement through an upper-division creative writing workshop (<a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/LIT-Literature/Upper-Division/LIT-179A'>LIT 179A</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/LIT-Literature/Upper-Division/LIT-179B'>LIT 179B</a>, or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/LIT-Literature/Upper-Division/LIT-179C'>LIT 179C</a>); they will fulfill the Media Critical Approaches requirement through the creative writing senior project seminar (<a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/LIT-Literature/Upper-Division/LIT-190V'>LIT 190V</a> or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/LIT-Literature/Upper-Division/LIT-190W'>LIT 190W</a>) if they choose the Senior Seminar option to fulfill the comprehensive requirement.</p>

<p>The course descriptions in the General Catalog specify the critical approaches satisfied by each course.</p>

<p>These courses may also satisfy distribution requirements (below).</p>

<p>A list of annual course offerings indicating critical approaches and distribution codes for each course is available in the department office and on <a href="https://literature.ucsc.edu/courses/index.php" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">the Literature Department website</a>.</p>
