								<h1>
	Chemistry B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><p>The requirements for the bachelor of arts (B.A.) in chemistry have been kept to a minimum so that students may tailor their program to their own purposes, for example, to pursue a double major, to complete major requirements late in their college career, or to concentrate on a specific branch of chemistry. The minimum requirements (including prerequisites) constitute 54 percent of a student&rsquo;s total undergraduate program; consequently, there is ample time to explore and discover other interests within the university. To plan wisely, students are advised to refer to each course description for a detailed listing of prerequisites. Students who decide they want a professional career in chemistry are advised to meet with the chemistry undergraduate faculty adviser.</p>
<h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p class="sc-BodyText">The chemistry and biochemistry adviser provides counsel to undergraduate majors. Students are encouraged to seek out advising and assistance in planning their academic career to ensure completion of their major in a timely manner. For assistance, please contact Chemistry Advising at&nbsp;<a href="mailto:chemistryadvising@ucsc.edu">chemistryadvising@ucsc.edu</a>&nbsp;or visit our <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.chemistry.ucsc.edu/academics/undergraduates/index.html" target="_blank">department website</a>. Transfer students should also consult the Transfer Information and Policy Section below.</p><table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Getting Started in the Major
		</h3><p><strong>High School Preparation</strong></p>

<p>Prospective chemistry majors should have a solid foundation in high school mathematics; familiarity with algebra, logarithms, trigonometry, and analytic geometry is particularly required. Students taking chemistry at UCSC begin with <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/CHEM-Chemistry-and-Biochemistry/0-99/CHEM-1A'>CHEM 1A</a> or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/CHEM-Chemistry-and-Biochemistry/0-99/CHEM-1B'>CHEM 1B</a>, both of which require a strong background in high school chemistry.</p>

<p><strong>Prerequisites</strong></p>

<p>In order to determine what preparation may be needed to succeed in the general chemistry sequence , <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/CHEM-Chemistry-and-Biochemistry/0-99/CHEM-1A'>CHEM 1A</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/CHEM-Chemistry-and-Biochemistry/0-99/CHEM-1B'>CHEM 1B</a>, and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/CHEM-Chemistry-and-Biochemistry/0-99/CHEM-1C'>CHEM 1C</a>, students are encouraged&nbsp;to take the&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="https://www.chemistry.ucsc.edu/academics/undergraduates/First%20Year%20Students/chemistry_self_assessment.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Chemistry Self-Assessment&nbsp;Examination</a>.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>Learning outcomes summarize the most important knowledge, skills, abilities, and attitudes that students are expected to develop over the course of their studies.</p>

<p>The program learning outcomes clearly communicate the faculty&rsquo;s expectations to students, provide a framework for faculty evaluation of the curriculum based on empirical data, and help improve and measure the impact of implemented changes.</p>

<p>Students graduating with a B.S. or B.A. in chemistry or a B.S. in biochemistry and molecular biology should be able to:</p>

<ul>
	<li>demonstrate mastery of a broad set of chemical knowledge concerning the fundamentals in the basic areas of the discipline (organic, inorganic, analytical, physical, and biochemistry);</li>
	<li>solve area-specific problems by identifying the essential parts of a problem, formulating a strategy for solving the problem, applying appropriate techniques to arrive at a solution, testing the correctness of the solution, and interpreting their results;</li>
	<li>use modern library search tools (such as SciFinder, PubMed, or Web of Science) to locate and retrieve scientific information about a topic, chemical, chemical technique, or an issue relating to chemistry;</li>
	<li>know and follow the proper procedures and regulations for safe handling and use of chemicals;</li>
	<li>understand the objective of their chemical experiments, properly carry out the experiments, and appropriately record and analyze the results;</li>
	<li>use computers in data acquisition and processing, and use available software as a tool in data analysis;</li>
	<li>use standard laboratory equipment, modern instrumentation, and classical techniques to carry out experiments;</li>
	<li>communicate the concepts and results of their laboratory experiments through effective writing and/or oral communication using the discipline standards for reporting and citation; and</li>
	<li>collaborate effectively as part of a team to solve problems, debate different points of view, and interact productively with a diverse group of team members.</li>
</ul>
