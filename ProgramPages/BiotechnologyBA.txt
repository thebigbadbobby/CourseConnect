								<h1>
	Biotechnology B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>The Bachelor of Arts in Biotechnology is intended for students who plan to be involved in the biotechnology industry as writers, artists, ethicists, executives, sales force, regulators, lawyers, politicians, and other roles that require an understanding of the technology, but not the intensive training needed for technicians, research scientists, engineers, and bioinformaticians. (For those more technical roles, the biomolecular engineering and bioinformatics major or the molecular, cell, and developmental biology major is recommended.)</p>

<p>The major is deliberately designed to be suitable as a double major for students in the humanities or social sciences.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>The Baskin School of Engineering <a href="https://undergrad.soe.ucsc.edu" target="_blank">Undergraduate Advising office</a> offers general advising for prospective and declared undergraduates majoring in School of Engineering programs. The office handles major declarations, transfer credits, course substitutions, articulations, and degree certifications. Undergraduate students obtain and submit all paperwork requiring departmental approval to the undergraduate advising office.&nbsp;Transfer students should also refer to the Transfer Information and&nbsp;Policy section.</p>

<p>Baskin Engineering Building, Room 225<br />
<a href="mailto:advising@soe.ucsc.edu">advising@soe.ucsc.edu</a><br />
(831) 459-5840</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Getting Started in the Major
		</h3><p>Students applying for admission as first-year students proposing to take biotechnology should have completed high-school biology.&nbsp;Any UC-eligible student with a strong interest in biotechnlogy is welcome in the program.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>A biotechnology student completing the program should</p>

<ul>
	<li>have familiarity with several different biotechnologies;</li>
	<li>be able to find and use information from a variety of sources, including books, journal articles, and online encyclopedias;</li>
	<li>be able to communicate clearly in writing, orally, and as posters; and</li>
	<li>be able to apply ethical reasoning to make decisions about biotechnology in a global, economic, environmental, and societal context.</li>
</ul>
<table>

			Major Qualification Policy and Declaration Process

			Major Qualification

<p>The portfolios must be turned in electronically as PDF files by the last day of the quarter of graduation, and will be reviewed quarterly by the undergraduate director.</p>

<p>Portfolios must contain the following:</p>

<ul>
	<li>A substantial written report on a project.&nbsp;This is typically satisfied by&nbsp;the&nbsp;capstone project report.</li>
	<li>Slides from a substantial verbal presentation.</li>
	<li>A poster. This is typically satisfied by a&nbsp;capstone design project poster, presented at the undergraduate poster symposium.</li>
</ul>

<p>The three parts of the portfolio should represent at least two different projects.&nbsp;At least one of the submissions must be from the capstone course.</p>

<p>Exit interviews are scheduled during the last week of the quarter by the Baskin School of Engineering (BSOE) advising office, generally as small group interviews.</p>

SI can be met by <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/BME-Biomolecular-Engineering/Lower-Division/BME-18'>BME 18</a>.<br />
TA can be met by several electives: <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/BME-Biomolecular-Engineering/100/BME-122H'>BME 122H</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/BME-Biomolecular-Engineering/100/BME-132'>BME 132</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/BME-Biomolecular-Engineering/100/BME-177'>BME 177</a>, or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/BME-Biomolecular-Engineering/100/BME-178'>BME 178</a>.</p>
