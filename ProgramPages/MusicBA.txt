								<h1>
	Music B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>The Music Department supports an unusually diverse curriculum for a department of its size. By a choice of concentrations within the bachelor of arts (B.A.) degree, the student can emphasize different aspects of music. The &ldquo;Contemporary Practices&rdquo;&nbsp; &ldquo;Global Musics&rdquo; concentrations offer a variety of modules (groupings of courses with various focuses) that students will be required to select from in addition to core requirements designed specifically for each concentration.&nbsp;All students pursuing the B.A. degree must select one of the three concentrations.&nbsp;</p>

<p>The concentrations and corresponding modules relevant to each concentration are:</p>

<p><strong>Contemporary Practices Concentration.</strong>&nbsp;A concentration emphasizing composition and improvisation across multiple genres of music composition.&nbsp;Modules offered are:&nbsp;</p>

<ul style="margin-left:40px">
	<li>Global Art Musics&nbsp;</li>
	<li>
	<p>Spontaneous Composition and Improvisation&nbsp;</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Experimental &amp; Contemporary Musics.&nbsp;This module offers a variety of electronic music studio courses.</p>
	</li>
</ul>

<p>All module offerings are based on availability within the curriculum plan. Students should visit the <a href="http://music.ucsc.edu" target="_blank">Music Department website</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;for a list of when each module&rsquo;s courses will be offered.&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Global Musics Concentration. </strong>A concentration emphasizing global music research, performance, and writing.&nbsp;&nbsp;Modules offered are:&nbsp;</p>

<ul style="margin-left:40px">
	<li>
	<p>Africa and the Americas</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Asia</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Contemporary/Experimental&nbsp;</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Jazz&nbsp;</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Popular Music&nbsp;</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>World Music&nbsp;</p>
	</li>
</ul>

<p>All module offerings are based on availability within the curriculum plan. Students should visit the <a href="http://music.ucsc.edu" target="_blank">Music Department website</a> for a list of when each module&rsquo;s courses will be offered.&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Western Art Music Concentration.</strong>&nbsp;A concentration emphasizing classical Western art music performance, theory and history. This concentration requires some performance ability on a standard orchestral instrument or voice in classical repertoire (or improvisational repertoire for drumset majors) upon entry to the concentration. This concentration does not have a set of modules, but rather has a set course list of requirements.&nbsp;</p>

<p>The list of course requirements and when the courses are offered can be found on the <a href="http://music.ucsc.edu" target="_blank">Music Department website</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>General information about the music degrees is available on<a href="http://music.ucsc.edu/"> the department website</a>. Students are also strongly encouraged to consult early with the Music Department adviser to create an academic plan for the major or a minor far in advance of declaration, as early as the summer before beginning at the university.</p>
<p>Transfer students should consult the Transfer Information and Policy section.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Please contact the department adviser by emailing <a rel="noopener noreferrer" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="mailto:music@ucsc.edu" target="_blank">music@ucsc.edu</a> or calling (831) 459-2292.&nbsp;<a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://slugsuccess.ucsc.edu/index.html" target="_blank">Slug Success</a> is also available to make an appointment.</p><table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Getting Started in the Major
		</h3><p>It is essential that frosh students plan to take <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-30A'>MUSC 30A</a>, as well as begin participating in ensembles in the fall of their second year, or as early as possible, in order to make satisfactory progress toward their degree objectives.</p>
<p>The theory placement exam is for placement of students, including transferring or re-entering students, into the appropriate music core course (<a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-13'>MUSC 13</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-14'>MUSC 14</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-15'>MUSC 15</a>, or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-30A'>MUSC 30A</a>). It includes written sections in the areas of theory and musicianship that emphasize aural recognition and identification of musical structures (intervals, chords, rhythms, meters, etc.). Students may prepare for the exam by honing skills in reading both bass and treble clef and in recognizing melodic and harmonic structures, and complex rhythmic patterns.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Students with some theory background should plan to take the theory placement exam to place into <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-13'>MUSC 13</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-14'>MUSC 14</a> and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-15'>MUSC 15</a>, or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-30A'>MUSC 30A</a>. Students without any theory background, or weak aural skills, should enroll in <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-13'>MUSC 13</a>, or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-14'>MUSC 14</a> and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-15'>MUSC 15</a> in their first year in order to prepare for the placement exam. Admission to <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-30A'>MUSC 30A</a> is primarily based on the theory placement exam scores, but students who get a final grade of &ldquo;A&rdquo; from the preparatory music theory classes (<a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-14'>MUSC 14</a> and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-15'>MUSC 15</a>) are eligible to automatically place into <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MUSC-Music/Lower-Division/MUSC-30A'>MUSC 30A</a>.</p>
<p>The theory placement exam is held twice a year: on the Tuesday before instruction begins in fall quarter, from 10:00 a.m.-12:30 p.m., and at the beginning of spring quarter (schedule TBA), both in the Music Center Recital Hall. No signup is necessary. Students should bring a pencil. Students can visit the Music Department website to view<a rel="noopener noreferrer" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="http://music.ucsc.edu/programs/undergraduate" target="_blank"> the sample exam</a>.&nbsp;</p><table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>The bachelor of arts&nbsp;program in music is designed to accomplish the following learning outcomes:</p>

<ol>
	<li>
	<p>Attainment of knowledge and understanding of music in an integrated way, through historical and cultural studies, musicianship, theory, composition, and performance. These studies prepare students to pursue a career in some aspect of music, to further develop their skills in graduate study, and/or to maintain music as a central part of their lives.</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Acquisition of musical competency, including literacy (the use of music notation in reading, performing, composing, analyzing, and hearing music).</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Developing skills of critical thinking and writing about music by taking courses on past and present musical cultures in a variety of global heritages.</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Creating music by performing, composing, and improvising.</p>
	</li>
</ol>

			Double Majors and Major/Minor Combinations Policy
		</h3><p>Double majors <strong>are</strong> possible for the music major. A student can double major with a Music Bachelor of Arts&nbsp;(B.A.) Degree or a Bachelor of Music (B.M.) Degree, and a major from another department. Students that plan to double major should meet with the music undergraduate adviser for course planning in fall quarter of their first year.&nbsp;</p>

Similarly, the &quot;Electronic Music Minor&quot; cannot be added to the Contemporary Practices concentration.</p>

<p>The &quot;Jazz, Spontaneous Composition and Improvisation Minor&quot; can be added to the Western Art Music concentration. The &quot;Electronic Music Minor&quot; can still be added to the Global Musics or Western Art Music concentration.</p>

See the music B.M. page for more information.</p>

    <p>In required and elective seminars, critical thinking and writing are assessed when students present a major independent project at the end of term.</p>
