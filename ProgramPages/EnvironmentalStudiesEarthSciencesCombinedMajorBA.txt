								<h1>
	Environmental Studies/Earth Sciences Combined Major B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>This course of study provides students with the basic tools of Earth sciences and environmental studies needed to address environmental problems.</p>

<p>Students interested in the available concentrations should choose to pursue the environmental studies B.A. degree. This does not apply to students who declared a combined major and concentration in agroecology and sustainable food systems prior to 2019.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>Advising is one way to make the most of your university experience. The advising system at UC Santa Cruz is amazing, and we encourage you to use it often. Ask questions, seek advice, and make decisions that work best for you.</p>

<p>To receive advising for this major, contact <a href="mailto:envsadvi@ucsc.edu">envsadvi@ucsc.edu</a>. Additional information for prospective transfer students can be found in the Transfer Information and Policy section.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>Students graduating with a B.A. degree in environmental studies/Earth sciences combined will be able to:</p>

<ul>
	<li>
	<p>Identify the societal (social, political, economic and ethical) agents and structures that contribute to environmental change. (social science competency)</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Describe the structure and functioning of major physical and ecological components of the earth&rsquo;s systems. (natural science competency)</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Access and analyze a complex literature addressing specific topics in environmental studies, and evaluate the usefulness and limitations of individual sources of information. (analytic thinking)</p>
	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Demonstrate effective oral and written communication skills. (communication skills).</p>
	</li>
</ul>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Major Qualification Policy and Declaration Process
		</h3><a id="mqual"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Major Qualification
		</h4><p>To qualify to declare the environmental studies/Earth sciences combined major, students must complete the specific courses listed below, or their approved equivalents.</p>
<table>

		</table><h5 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading3">
			One of the following
		</h5><table>

			</tr><tr>
				<td class="sc-coursenumber"><a class="sc-courselink" href="/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/BIOE-Biology-Ecology-and-Evolutionary/Lower-Division/BIOE-20C">BIOE 20C</a></td><td class="sc-coursetitle">Ecology and Evolution</td><td><p class="credits">5</p></td>
			</tr>
		</table><h5 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading3">
			Plus all of the following
		</h5><table>
			<tr>
				<td class="sc-coursenumber"><a class="sc-courselink" href="/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ENVS-Environmental-Studies/Lower-Division/ENVS-25">ENVS 25</a></td><td class="sc-coursetitle">Environmental Policy and Economics</td><td><p class="credits">5</p></td>

	<li>

	</li>
	<li>
	<p>Students with AP credit for any of the qualification course(s) are eligible to declare after successfully completing the remaining qualification courses.</p>
	</li>

	<p>Students must attend an Environmental Studies Department declaration workshop when requesting to declare the major.</p>

			Honors
		</h3><p><strong>Departmental Honors. </strong>Students must have a 3.5 grade point average (GPA) in all courses used to satisfy the environmental studies upper-division requirements. To be considered for departmental honors, students are limited to no more than one grade of P in those upper-division courses.</p>

<p><strong>Senior Comprehensive Honors.</strong> Only applicable to a senior thesis, senior internship, or individual work in a senior seminar. Honors must be awarded by the student&rsquo;s faculty sponsor, and a second faculty member (chosen by the student&rsquo;s faculty adviser) must confer.</p>

<p><strong>Highest Departmental Honors.</strong> Students must have a 3.75 grade point average (GPA) in in all courses used to satisfy the environmental studies upper-division requirements and must also receive senior comprehensive honors (see above). To be considered for highest departmental&nbsp;honors, students are limited to no more than one grade of P in those upper-division courses.</p>

<p>Students must fulfill all requirements for honors from environmental studies and biology.</p>
