								<h1>
	Computer Science: Computer Game Design B.S.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>The goal of this degree is to provide students a deep understanding of the technical aspects of computer game engineering and a broad background in the artistic, systemic, and production elements of game design and development. The core of the degree program is a strong grounding in computer science and computer engineering, preceded by a foundation in mathematics. Classes also develop skills in areas such as visual communication and team-oriented game production, while developing knowledge of topics such as game&nbsp;history, play experiences, game systems, and social and ethical issues. In their upper-division courses, students gain depth by taking electives in computational media and computer science and engineering, with options such as Game AI, Game Graphics and Real-Time Rendering, Mobile Applications, and Interactive Storytelling. A two-course interdisciplinary Game Development Experience&nbsp;and a yearlong interdisciplinary capstone Game Design Studio class allow students to develop substantial computer games and integrate materials from the rest of the program.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>The Baskin School of Engineering (BSOE) Undergraduate Advising Office is located in the Baskin Engineering Building,&nbsp;Room 225. It can be contacted by email at <a href="mailto:advising@soe.ucsc.edu.">advising@soe.ucsc.edu</a>&nbsp;or at the <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://undergrad.soe.ucsc.edu" target="_blank">Undergraduate Advising website</a>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Transfer students to the program should consult the <a rel="noopener noreferrer" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://undergrad.soe.ucsc.edu/transfer-students" target="_blank">Transfer Students section</a> of the Baskin Engineering Undergraduate Affairs page.</p><table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Getting Started in the Major
		</h3><p>It is recommended that high school students intending to apply to the major have completed four years of mathematics (through advanced algebra and trigonometry) as well as any available courses in computer science, arts, and media (especially interactive media). Comparable college mathematics, arts, and media courses completed at other institutions also serve to properly prepare a student for the major.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>Recipients of a B.S. degree in Computer Game Design at UC Santa Cruz&nbsp;are expected to have the following skills and experiences:</p>

<ol>
	<li>Demonstrate mastery of computer science in the following core knowledge areas: algorithms, data structures, complexity, and software engineering and development.</li>
	<li>Apply system-level perspective by thinking at multiple levels of detail and abstraction and by recognizing the context in which a computer system may function, including its interactions with people and the physical world.</li>
	<li>Apply problem-solving skills and the knowledge of computer science to solve real problems.</li>
	<li>Recognize and take into account the social, legal, ethical, and cultural issues in the discipline of computer games.</li>
	<li>Demonstrate written and oral communication skills regarding technical material about computer science and computer games, broadly conceived.</li>
	<li>Design and build a technical system that achieves an artistic goal for audience experience, employing sound computer science techniques.</li>
	<li>Demonstrate the ability to collaboratively plan, organize, and execute complex, team-oriented projects, using appropriate communication and coordination techniques.</li>
</ol>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Major Qualification Policy and Declaration Process
		</h3><a id="mqual"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Major Qualification
		</h4><p>In order to be admitted into the Computer Science: Computer Game Design major, students must be listed as a proposed major within the School of Engineering. Please refer to <a rel="noopener noreferrer" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://undergrad.soe.ucsc.edu/prepare-declare-bsoe-major" target="_blank">https://undergrad.soe.ucsc.edu/prepare-declare-bsoe-major</a>.</p>
<p>Transfers to the program should consult the <a rel="noopener noreferrer" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://undergrad.soe.ucsc.edu/transfer-students" target="_blank">Transfer Students section</a> of the Baskin Engineering Undergraduate Affairs page.</p>

		</h3><a id="4yrplan"></a><p>The following are three sample academic plans that students can use to plan their sequence of courses in the major. The first two plans are suggested guidelines for students who begin their studies in their frosh year. Such students, if they plan carefully, will have several openings free to take other breadth courses they find interesting.</p>

<p>Plan one is for a student entering UCSC in their frosh year who is prepared to go directly into <a class='sc-courselink' href='/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MATH-Mathematics/Lower-Division/MATH-19A'>MATH 19A</a>/<a class='sc-courselink' href='/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MATH-Mathematics/Lower-Division/MATH-20A'>MATH 20A</a> and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/CSE-Computer-Science-and-Engineering/Lower-Division/CSE-30'>CSE 30</a>.</p>

<p>Plan two is for a student entering UCSC their frosh year who needs to take preparatory courses prior to <a class='sc-courselink' href='/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/MATH-Mathematics/Lower-Division/MATH-19A'>MATH 19A</a> or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/CSE-Computer-Science-and-Engineering/Lower-Division/CSE-30'>CSE 30</a> to ensure a successful outcome in those courses.</p>

<p>Plan three is for a junior transfer student who has completed their mathematics, computational foundations, and general education requirements.</p>
