</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>For sociology-related advising inquiries contact:</p>

<p><a href="mailto:socy@ucsc.edu">socy@ucsc.edu</a><br />
831-459-4888</p>

<p>Visit the sociology website for <a href="https://sociology.ucsc.edu/undergraduate/advising/index.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">additional advising information</a>, including&nbsp;advising hours, appointments, and peer advising.</p>

<p>Sociology advising is located in Rachel Carson College Academic Building, Rm 226.</p>

<p>Transfer students must also review the Transfer Information and Policy&nbsp;section.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>Students graduating with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Sociology will:&nbsp;</p>

<p>1. Critical Thinking: Demonstrate critical thinking skills by analyzing and evaluating social, political, and/or cultural arguments, across a variety of areas such as inequality; social problems; and race, class, and gender.</p>

<p>2. Sociological Understanding: Demonstrate sociological understandings of phenomena, for example, how individual biographies are shaped by social structures, social institutions, cultural practices, and multiple axes of difference and/or inequality.</p>

<p>3. Written and Oral Communication: Formulate effective and convincing written and/or oral arguments.</p>

<p>4. Social Theory: Demonstrate the ability to use several of the major classical and/or contemporary perspectives in social theory.</p>

<p>5. Research Methodology: Demonstrate the ability to use several of the major social science research methodologies.</p>

<p>To ensure timely progress in the major, transfer students admitted as proposed sociology majors must take <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/SOCY-Sociology/Lower-Division/SOCY-3A'>SOCY 3A</a> in fall quarter if they have not satisfied the articulated equivalent at their previous institution. If <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/SOCY-Sociology/Lower-Division/SOCY-3A'>SOCY 3A</a> has been completed prior to transfer, it is recommended that <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/SOCY-Sociology/Upper-Division/SOCY-105A'>SOCY 105A</a> be taken in fall or winter quarter. In addition, transfer students who are pursuing the GISES intensive concentration must take <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/SOCY-Sociology/Lower-Division/SOCY-30A'>SOCY 30A</a> in fall quarter as it is the start of a yearlong course series.</p>

Up to five relevant courses taken through study abroad programs from which credits are transferable to UCSC may be used toward satisfaction of the sociology B.A. with a concentration in GISES intensive major elective requirements when the content is deemed appropriate and approved by the Everett/GISES program faculty director.</p>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Honors
		</h3><p>The Sociology Department awards honors in the major based on the student&rsquo;s cumulative GPA for all courses taken to satisfy the program&rsquo;s major requirements, excluding the comprehensive requirement. Students with a GPA of 3.75 or above will be considered for honors in the major. Students with a GPA of 3.9 or above will be considered for highest honors in the major. No more than approximately 15 percent of the graduating class will be considered for honors or highest honors in the major. Comprehensive honors is awarded to students who complete the senior thesis option, and their faculty thesis sponsor and one additional reader evaluate the thesis to be of honors quality.</p>

		<h2>
			Sociology with Concentration in GISES Intensive Major
		</h2><p>The GISES&nbsp;intensive concentration&nbsp;is an option for students wishing to major in sociology and focus in the area of Global Information and Social Enterprise Studies (GISES). The major is sponsored and administered by the Department of Sociology. This major is designed for highly motivated, self-directed and enterprising students who have demonstrated the capacity to design and complete an honors-quality project for a civil society group. Students are required to take a total of 15 courses (three prescribed lower-division courses in preparation for the major, two prescribed lower-division core courses, two prescribed upper-division GISES courses, two prescribed upper-division core courses, five upper-division electives, and a project practicum course). In addition, they must successfully complete the comprehensive requirement prior to graduation.</p>

<p>In addition to the specific courses shown in these planners, a student must complete courses satisfying the CC, ER, IM, MF, SI, TA, PR and composition general education (GE) requirements. All sociology majors are required to take a statistical methods course which satisfies the SR GE, and GISES students complete <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/SOCY-Sociology/Lower-Division/SOCY-30A'>SOCY 30A</a> which satisfies the PE GE. Other GEs that could be satisfied in the sociology major, but are not required, are CC, ER, and TA.</p>
