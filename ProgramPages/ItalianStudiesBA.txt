								<h1>
	Italian Studies B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>Students interested in an interdisciplinary approach to Italian culture through the combined study of language, literature, history, art history, and other subjects may pursue a major in Italian studies. A <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://italianstudies.ucsc.edu/academics/italian-studies-checklist-major.pdf" target="_blank">detailed checklist</a> for the major is available on the <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://italianstudies.ucsc.edu/academics/index.html" target="_blank">Italian studies program web site</a>.</p>
<p>Staff advisers for the Italian studies major are available in the Literature Department office, located in Humanities 1, room 303. The Italian studies program is administered by the Literature Department.</p><table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p class="sc-BodyText">Students should meet with an adviser for Italian Studies program for assistance in making a plan for completion of the Italian studies major requirements.</p>
<p class="sc-BodyText">Advisers for the Italian Studies program are available in the Literature Department office, located in Humanities 1, room 303, and may also be contacted at <a href="mailto:litdept@ucsc.edu">litdept@ucsc.edu</a> or (831) 459-4778.</p>
<p class="sc-BodyText">Transfer students should also consult the Transfer Information and Policy section of the program statement.</p><table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>Following is a summary of the program learning outcomes for the Italian studies Bachelor of Arts (B.A.).</p>

<p>Students who complete the major should be able to demonstrate:</p>

<ol>
	<li><strong>Oral and written communication:</strong> listening, speaking, and writing competency in Italian sufficient to engage in meaningful conversation at the intermediate-high to advanced-mid level, according to the <a href="https://www.actfl.org/" target="_blank">American Council on the Teaching of Foreign Languages (ACTFL)</a>.</li>
	<li><strong>Textual comprehension and analysis:</strong> ability to understand and appreciate Italian texts in a variety of registers and different areas of knowledge at the advanced level as described by <a href="https://www.actfl.org/" target="_blank">ACTFL</a>.</li>
	<li><strong>Critical analysis:</strong> ability to comment with critical insight on a range of topics in Italian history and contemporary culture.</li>
	<li><strong>Cultural competency:</strong> ability to demonstrate knowledge of historical and contemporary aspects of Italian culture with reference to study in several disciplines.</li>
	<li><strong>Global engagement:</strong> ability to recognize and reflect in an informed way on cultural differences as well as shared values between Italian culture and their own native cultures.</li>
	<li><strong>Knowledge building:</strong> ability to conduct research in both Italian and English on topics pertinent to Italian history and culture.</li>
</ol>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Major Qualification Policy and Declaration Process
		</h3><a id="mqual"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Major Qualification
		</h4><p>Undeclared students may declare the major at any time. While specific courses are not required in order to declare, students will have ideally completed some or all of the lower-division language requirements.</p><a id="decproc"></a>
<table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			How to Declare a Major
		</h4><p>Declaring a major or minor in Italian studies is a three-step process:</p>

<ol>
	<li>Print a <a href="https://advising.ucsc.edu/planning/your-major/declaration/" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">petition</a> for the major or minor and fill it out to the best of your ability. Be sure to indicate your expected graduation term (EGT) on the petition. Your EGT is visible in your student advising summary or on your My.UCSC student portal.</li>
	<li>Meet with the Italian studies program director in order to discuss your plans and fill out a major or minor checklist.</li>
	<li>Meet with an Italian studies staff adviser in order to review and complete your declaration form. In order to schedule an appointment with a staff adviser, email <a href="mailto:litdept@ucsc.edu">litdept@ucsc.edu</a>, call (831) 459-4778, or come to the Literature Department office (Humanities 1, room 303).</li>
</ol><p /><table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Transfer Information and Policy
		</h3><a id="tasp"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Transfer Admission Screening Policy
		</h4><p>Students planning to apply&nbsp;in the Italian studies major are not required to complete specific courses for consideration of admission to UC Santa Cruz.</p>

<p>Transfer students are advised to complete two years of Italian language study before enrolling at UCSC. It is also recommended that students complete courses satisfying campus general education requirements or the Intersegmental General Education Transfer Curriculum (IGETC) before coming to UCSC.</p>
<table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Getting Started at UCSC as a Transfer Student
		</h4><p>Transfer students take a language placement test when they come to UC Santa Cruz. If a student joining UCSC is determined (through the placement test) to have completed the equivalent of UCSC&#39;s <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-1'>ITAL 1</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-2'>ITAL 2</a> and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-3'>ITAL 3</a>, they can complete the major in two years as shown in the planners for the major&nbsp;(Transfer Plan A). This requires completion of at least one year of Italian language in community college unless a transfer student is a heritage speaker or has studied Italian extensively at the high school level. If a transfer student is determined to have completed the equivalent of <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-6'>ITAL 6</a>, they can follow Transfer Plan B, which is more flexible. Students entering UCSC in winter quarter should have completed the equivalent of UCSC&#39;s <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-1'>ITAL 1</a>-<a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-4'>ITAL 4</a>.</p>

<p>Students who have not completed Italian language study before coming to UC Santa Cruz should contact the staff adviser for the program after coming to UCSC to plan their program. For instance, a transfer student entering UCSC in the fall quarter can complete <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-1'>ITAL 1</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-2'>ITAL 2</a> and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-3'>ITAL 3</a> and the five courses taught in English that are required for the major in their first year, followed by intensive Italian in the summer term (e.g., UC Berkeley&#39;s language immersion courses), allowing them to take the five required courses taught in Italian during their second year. A transfer student entering UCSC in the winter quarter could take a two-quarter intensive Italian course in winter and spring that is the equivalent of <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-1'>ITAL 1</a>, <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-2'>ITAL 2</a>, and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/ITAL-Italian/Lower-Division/ITAL-3'>ITAL 3</a>.</p>

		</h3><p>This program does not have a letter grade policy.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Study Abroad
		</h3><p>There are several options for study in Italy through the UC Education Abroad Program (UCEAP), either for a year (Bologna, Milan), for an intensive semester or quarter (Bologna, Florence, Milan, Rome), or for the summer (Florence, Rome). Students may also divide their time between Italy and Spain for one semester or one quarter. Please see <a href="https://studyabroad.ucsc.edu/" target="_blank">UC study abroad programs&nbsp;</a>for information.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Honors
		</h3><p>Honors in the Italian studies major are awarded to graduating seniors who have earned a 3.70 to 3.89 grade point average in their upper-division Italian studies courses. Highest honors in the Italian studies major are awarded to graduating seniors who have earned a 3.90 or higher grade point average in their upper-division Italian Studies courses. Honors are not awarded in the minor.</p>
