								<h1>
	Feminist Studies B.A.
</h1><div id="degree-req">
	<div id="degree-req-1">
		<h2>
			Information and Policies
		</h2><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Introduction
		</h3><p>Feminist studies is an interdisciplinary field of analysis in the humanities that investigates how relations of gender are embedded in social, political, and cultural formations. The undergraduate program in feminist studies provides students with a unique interdisciplinary and transnational perspective. The department emphasizes theories and practices derived from multiracial and multicultural contexts. Some topics you will find in our curriculum include: colonialism/postcolonialism; postsocialism; queer and trans theory; visual culture, legal studies; critical race theory; science and technology studies; and social movements.</p>

<p>Feminist studies prepares undergraduates for a variety of careers. The Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) degree in Feminist Studies provides excellent grounding for undergraduates who have career aspirations in, for example, law, health, non-governmental organizations, museum curation, politics, media and film, research institutes, journalism, community organizations, and social services.</p>

<p>Students wishing to pursue doctoral work will also find that interdisciplinary training in feminist studies equips them with theoretical and methodological strengths in most disciplines and applied research fields including American studies, ethnic studies, science studies, anthropology, communications, and legal studies. Specialists in feminist studies are employed as consultants in industry, higher education, and human resources. State and federal government agencies employ people who have special training in understanding gender and race relations. Educational institutions need specialists to develop and administer feminist studies programs, women&rsquo;s centers, and other institutional structures designed specifically to study and assist marginalized peoples, including women.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Academic Advising for the Program
		</h3><p>Email: <a href="mailto:fmst@ucsc.edu">fmst@ucsc.edu</a><br />
Phone: (831) 459-1478</p>

<p>Feminist Studies advising is held in Humanities 1, Room 415A. Drop in hours are posted on the Feminist Studies website. Students can make an appointment by using the Slug Success application found under Resources in their student portal (MyUCSC).&nbsp;</p>

<p>Transfer students should also consult the Transfer Student Information and Policy section for specific requirements.</p>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Program Learning Outcomes
		</h3><p>Students who complete the feminist studies major should emerge with the following skills, competencies, and knowledge:</p>

<p><strong>Outcome 1: Writing, Reading, and Critical Thought</strong></p>

<ol>
	<li>Develop writing skills and proficiency</li>
	<li>Perform close reading of texts</li>
	<li>Develop an ability to formulate and defend arguments in writing and oral presentation</li>
	<li>Develop media literacy with a lens of cross-cultural analysis</li>
	<li>Develop a critical understanding of inequities, ethics, racial and gender formations, and social justice issues</li>
</ol>

<p><strong>Outcome 2: Feminist Methods</strong></p>

<p>The lower-division classes focus on &ldquo;understanding&rdquo; and the upper-division courses, especially the senior seminars, focus on &ldquo;application.&rdquo;</p>

<ol>
	<li>Develop an understanding of feminist interdisciplinary methodologies</li>
	<li>Apply interdisciplinary methodology to research and writing</li>
</ol>
<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Major Qualification Policy and Declaration Process
		</h3><a id="mqual"></a><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Major Qualification
		</h4><p>Undeclared students may declare the feminist studies&nbsp;major at any time. While specific courses are not required in order to declare, students ideally will have completed or be enrolled in <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/FMST-Feminist-Studies/Lower-Division/FMST-1'>FMST 1</a>,&nbsp;Feminist Studies: An Introduction.</p><a id="decproc"></a>
<table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			How to Declare a Major
		</h4><p>For a single major, complete the <a rel="noopener noreferrer" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://advising.ucsc.edu/planning/your-major/declaration/docs/major-declaration.pdf" target="_blank">Petition for Major Declaration</a> form and sign it. For a double major or major/minor complete both the <a rel="noopener noreferrer" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://advising.ucsc.edu/planning/your-major/declaration/docs/major-declaration.pdf" target="_blank">Major Declaration form</a> and also an <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://advising.ucsc.edu/planning/apf.html" target="_blank">Academic Planning form</a>. Both of these forms must be signed by you and by each departmental adviser.</p>

<table>

		</table><h3 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading1">
			Honors
		</h3><p>Feminist studies awards honors and highest honors in the major. At the end of each quarter, a faculty committee meets to review graduating students&rsquo; files. Students are considered for honors and highest honors based on their cumulative GPA, calculated from grades earned in coursework and the comprehensive&nbsp;requirement undertaken for completion of the major. For honors, students must earn a minimum GPA of 3.70 in the relevant courses, while for highest honors, the GPA must be 3.90 or higher. Writing a thesis is not a requirement for receiving honors or highest honors.</p>

			Disciplinary Communication (DC) Requirement
		</h4><p>Students of every major must satisfy that major&rsquo;s upper-division Disciplinary Communication (DC) requirement. The DC requirement in feminist studies is satisfied by completing the comprehensive&nbsp;requirement (FMST 194 or <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/FMST-Feminist-Studies/Upper-Division/FMST-195'>FMST 195</a>).</p><table>

		</table><h4 class="sc-RequiredCoursesHeading2">
			Comprehensive Requirement
		</h4><p>Comprehensive&nbsp;requirement options include a senior seminar taught by core faculty or a senior thesis/project. Completion of the Entry Level Writing and Composition Requirements are prerequisites to FMST 194 and <a class='sc-courselink' href='/en/Current/General-Catalog/Courses/FMST-Feminist-Studies/Upper-Division/FMST-195'>FMST 195</a>.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Double majors&nbsp;may be able to write a thesis of 50 or more pages over three quarters to satisfy&nbsp;the comprehensive&nbsp;requirements of feminist studies and the second major. The Feminist Studies Department will review and approve or deny proposals for a double thesis on a case-by-case basis.</p>

<p>Students should be aware that not all departments will accept a double thesis. Students who wish to propose a double thesis should consult with both major advisers before the end of their junior year. Such a proposal must be approved by both departments or referred to the Committee on Educational Policy. In evaluating such proposals, the departments will require the student to have completed significantly more work than the minimum required for the comprehensive&nbsp;requirement of each major. The student&#39;s work must clearly explain to the reader the relevance of the work to both disciplines.</p>
